<h2><strong>MOE Financial Assistance Scheme (FAS)</strong></h2>
<h4>Note: For more information on the MOE FAS, please <a href="https://www.moe.gov.sg/financial-matters/financial-assistance" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">click here</a>.</h4>
<h3><strong>Who is eligible</strong></h3>
<p>Students who:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Are Singapore Citizens.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Attend government or government-aided schools.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Have a monthly gross household income of $3,000 or less, or a monthly
per capita income of $750 or less.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h3><strong>Difference between gross household income and per capita income</strong></h3>
<p>To calculate per capita income, divide the monthly gross household income
by the number of people living in the same household. This allows students
from larger families to benefit from the scheme.</p>
<h3><strong>How it works</strong></h3>
<p>Under MOE FAS, your child will receive:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>A full subsidy of standard miscellaneous fees. Singapore Citizens do not
need to pay school fees in primary schools.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Free textbooks and school attire.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>One of the following transport subsidies:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Covers 65% of school bus fares per year if your child takes the school
bus.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>$17 transport credit per month, or up to $204 per year, if your child
takes public transport.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p>A $2.60 per meal subsidy for school meals, applicable to 7 meals per school
week.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h3><strong>How to apply</strong></h3>
<p>You can apply&nbsp;<a href="https://form.gov.sg/68b7e92465cd36be287889c6" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">online</a>. Alternatively,
you can download the&nbsp;<a href="https://www.moe.gov.sg/api/media/1bf95534-1046-43c3-91ba-a1ebadefd16f/MOE-FAS-Application-Form.pdf" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">MOE FAS application form</a>&nbsp;or
collect a copy at your child’s school. Submit the completed form directly
to the school.</p>
<p><strong>Tip</strong>: If you have children in different government or
government-aided schools, you can submit 1 application form to any of their
schools. The school that receives the application form will inform you
of the outcome.</p>
<h3><strong>Additional financial help</strong></h3>
<p>If you require further financial assistance beyond the MOE FAS, you can
approach your child’s school to find out more about their school-based
financial assistance. Your request will be evaluated by the school on a
case-by-case basis.</p>
<p>Students from low-income households can apply for a subsidised computer,
broadband or both through the Infocomm Media Development Authority (IMDA)’s&nbsp;
<a href="http://www.digitalaccess.gov.sg/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">digital access programme</a>. If you have questions about the programme,
you can contact IMDA at&nbsp;<a href="tel:+6563773800" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">6377 3800</a>&nbsp;or&nbsp;<a href="mailto:info@imda.gov.sg" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">info@imda.gov.sg</a>.</p>
<p>Besides financial assistance schemes, MOE has merit-based awards and scholarships
that your child may also qualify.&nbsp;<a href="https://www.moe.gov.sg/financial-matters/awards-scholarships" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Learn about it</a>.</p>
